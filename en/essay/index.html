Founder's Essay

Why We CarryCraft Forward。We do not want Japanese culture to end with preservation alone. We want the skills and spirit of makers to be chosen in contemporary society and remain as something the next generation can admire.

It began with the discomfort that value was not reaching people.

Lacquer, bamboo, wood, ceramics. Japan has crafts that have been passed down over long periods of time. Yet in today's daily life, there are not many chances to encounter them. When people see them, they are moved. When they hold them, they can feel the accumulation of time. But if the places of encounter remain scarce, that value cannot reach society.

What Kameya Japan Tradition first faced was not simply arranging works, but increasing the opportunities for craft and people to meet. That is why the founder personally traveled across Japan, spoke with artists, and built trust one relationship at a time.

If there are too few opportunities to see craft, we create them. Everything begins there.

Creating a structure where artists' skills reach the right places.

The works of artists, including Living National Treasures, carry not only technique but a life itself. To avoid treating that value lightly, Kameya Japan Tradition values building trust before selling works.

Our aim is to create a flow where artists are properly compensated and that income leads to new production and the development of successors. When works are placed in the most appropriate settings, such as hotels, cultural facilities, companies, and overseas museums, craft becomes not only an object to view but a memory of space and experience.

Bringing culture into industry does not mean diluting it.

Turning traditional culture into business requires care. Instead of consuming it casually, we create points of contact where it can be chosen in society while maintaining respect for its background and techniques. The events, space design, and product planning of Kameya Japan Tradition are methods for doing that.

As craft enters corporate spaces, connects with regional stories, and resonates with cultures overseas, those accumulated efforts support makers' futures and become an entry point for younger generations to want to join this world.

Toward a culture that is not only preserved, but continuously chosen.

Culture does not continue when someone only protects it from one side. It moves into the next era little by little by being used, spoken about, placed in spaces, and remembered by people.

Together with artists, companies, regions, and overseas cultural institutions, Kameya Japan Tradition will create a state where Japanese craft resonates naturally in the world. Quietly, deeply, and surely, we continue taking steps toward the future.

Opening Japanese culture to society in a form suited to the age ahead.
