Shape Culture

In today's society,
the value of craft
is becoming harder to receive.

Many people are moved when they encounter its appeal.
Yet opportunities to meet it in daily life
and choose it remain limited.

Culture
does not continue simply because someone protects it.
It continues by being used, spoken about,
and handed down in society,
and by being connected to the next age.
That is what we believe.

When culture enters a space,
the atmosphere changes.

When craft is added to an experience,
a memorable moment is born.

When a business has a point of contact with culture,
its own outline begins to stand out.

Culture has that kind of power.

That is why culture from now on
needs not only people who receive it,
but people who co-create it.
That is what we believe.

Japanese Culture,
in a form suited to the age ahead,
opened to society.

We hope to nurture
that path together.
